“Architectural definition, in their surgical precision, reinforce and amplify the impossible alternatives: on the one hand, architecture as a thing of the mind, a dematerialized or conceptual discipline with its typological and morphological variations, and on the other, architecture as an empirical event that concentrates on the senses, on the experience of space” -- Bernard Tschumi

One year of study in ED2 opened my eyes and lead me into the design world. How to perceive the environment? How to extract the information you need when you go on a site? How to build relationships between exterior conditions, such as wind and sunlight, and interior space? How to use sketches and designs to articulate your intention? How to and what can define a threshold? These well-designed projects in ED 2 resonate with Bernard’s quote, which is “experience”.

Furthermore, two years of intense study in the ED3 and ED4 in architecture stream force me to believe that architectural drawing sketches and modeling are not only the ways of thinking for architects but also a means to communicate with others. It is “a thing of the mind”. What is architecture? What consists of architecture? Considerations of materiality, space, void, mass, texture, movement, system, forms, orders, structure, artistic, abstract, and poetic aspects are all the clues that lead to the architectural world.
